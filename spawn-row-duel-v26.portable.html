How to Play
The Ember Bastion
Your command center holds the base — it cannot move or attack, and no spell can raze or burn it. It falls only when a strike reaches it through the rows. If it falls, you lose.

Win — raze the command center. Each side anchors its base with a command center: the structure you chose at the start. It sets your starting health, your starting workers, and your mana colors. It cannot move or attack — and the moment it is destroyed, its owner loses. Everything else on the field exists to protect yours and reach theirs.

The board. Five rows stack between the two keeps: the enemy base and front line up top, your front line and base below, and a single contested center that belongs to no one. You may place into any of your three rows. Targeting is by row distance: adjacent strikes land directly; deeper strikes can be intercepted by defenders in any row they cross.

Workers & the rail. Each row's worker number is the running total of the worker stats of the cards in it — structures add (a Barracks might be ⚒+3), monsters subtract their upkeep (a Dragon ⚒−2), so a row holding both sits at ⚒+1. Workers are not trained and never move; they simply follow the cards. Tap a row's worker on the rail to Harvest the whole row at once (◆1 per worker at the base, ◆2 at the front or center); a pop-up then lets you distribute the haul across the colors among that row's units, with the remainder kept as generic ◆. At the start of your turn, any row whose number has gone negative (for example because a supporting structure was razed) must be brought back to 0 or higher by moving its monsters to a row that can support them, or sacrificing them — you resolve this before you can act. You may overextend on your own turn; the bill comes due at the start of the next.

Colored mana. Extraction yields generic ◆ — or mana of any color among your units in that row (a red dragon on the front line lets front workers pull red; your command center counts for its own colors in its row). A colored creature or spell needs 1 mana of its color in its cost; the rest takes any mix. Structures take anything. Mana of every kind drains at end of turn — bank it on face-downs to keep it.

Face-downs & banking. Setting a card is free. Pour mana into it across turns; when fully funded it flips (surplus banks on the card, visible to all). A face-down attacked mid-funding is interrupted and destroyed with its invested mana. Flips from a funded face-down waive the color pip — secrecy's discount. Face-down a full turn? It flips with haste.

Attack — alone or as a group. Tap ready creatures (same row to group), then tap an enemy unit, structure, face-down, worker stack, or the command center itself. The defender answers a traveling strike with interceptors from any row it crosses. Combat is simultaneous (First Strike aside) with no spillover.

Note: a prototype. Open questions, deliberately parked: the exact colored-cost rule (currently 1 pip + any remainder), storage structures that convert colors, a Ranged keyword, and the soldier-production model.
